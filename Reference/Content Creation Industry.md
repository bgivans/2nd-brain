# Content Creation Industry — Reference

> December 12, 2024. A 4-message session on the size and structure of the content creation industry. Quick market research. Useful for GG positioning and for understanding where content services sit as a market.

---

## The Numbers (as of late 2024)

**Global creator economy:**
- Estimated at $250–$480 billion depending on how "content creation" is defined
- Growing at 10-20% annually
- Over 200 million full or part-time content creators worldwide
- The top 1% make the money; the bottom 99% generate the ecosystem

**The relevant segment for GG:**
Not the influencer economy (top of funnel, brand deals, followers). The **B2B content production** market — businesses that pay for content creation as a service:
- Small business social media management: $5,000–$15,000/month for full-service agencies
- Freelance content creation: $50–$500/post depending on quality and platform
- Content strategy consulting: $150–$500/hour

**The AI content disruption:**
By late 2024, AI was already compressing the low end of this market. Businesses that would have paid $500/month for 20 basic posts were finding they could generate those themselves for $20/month in AI tools. The market isn't disappearing — it's stratifying.

---

## The Stratification

| Tier | What They Pay For | AI Impact |
|------|------------------|-----------|
| High-end ($5K+/mo) | Strategy + execution + brand management | Low — the strategy layer is still human |
| Mid-market ($1-5K/mo) | Content strategy + production | Medium — AI handles production, humans handle strategy |
| Low-end (<$1K/mo) | Volume posting, generic content | High — most of this is being automated or DIY'd |

**The opportunity:** GG is positioned in the mid-to-high tier — not producing volume content, but building content *systems* that enable volume. The system install ($2,000) + retainer ($200-300/mo) model sits right at the stratification line where AI augments the human, not replaces it.

---

## Why This Matters for GG Positioning

GG's content services aren't "content creation" in the creator economy sense. They're **content infrastructure** — the system that makes consistent, on-brand content production possible for a business that doesn't have a content team.

The positioning: *"I don't make content for you. I build the system so your team (or AI) can make content that actually looks and sounds like you."*
