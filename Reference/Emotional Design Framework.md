# Emotional Design Framework

> Strategy first. Visuals second. Always.

Extracted from studying Anastasia / @girlinbluestudios. The goal is to steal the sequencing — not the aesthetic.

---

## The Three-Step Sequence

### Step 1 — Emotional Audit
Before naming. Before colors. Before anything.

- What does the customer feel **before** finding you?
- What do you want them to feel **after** working with you?
- What's the gap between those two states? That gap is your brand's job.

### Step 2 — Utility-First Naming
The name should earn its cleverness by also being accurate.

- Does the name communicate what the product actually does?
- If there's a pun or wordplay — does it still land if you strip the cleverness?
- Test: could someone describe the business just from the name alone?

### Step 3 — Cohesion Check
Name, visual identity, and marketing message should all point at the same emotional promise.

- Does the visual language match the emotional audit outcome?
- Does the marketing copy reinforce the name's implied promise?
- If any of the three point in a different direction — find the break and fix it
