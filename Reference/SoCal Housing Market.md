# SoCal Housing Market — Reference

> June 13, 2025. A 14-message session analyzing Southern California home affordability trends. Directly relevant to the North Star (Seal Beach goal) and long-term financial planning.

---

## The Context

This session was analyzing: *Can someone at Bryce's income level realistically afford to buy in SoCal, and on what timeline?*

The answer in June 2025 — and still largely true in March 2026 — is: **not yet, but the math is mappable.**

---

## SoCal Affordability Snapshot (2025)

**San Diego County:**
- Median home price: $850,000–$950,000 (June 2025)
- 20% down on median: $170,000–$190,000
- Monthly payment at 7% on $720K: ~$4,800/month (principal + interest only)
- Required income for that payment (28% rule): ~$205,000/year

**Orange County (Seal Beach area):**
- Median home price: $900,000–$1.1M
- Higher than San Diego — Seal Beach is a premium coastal pocket
- The beach-access premium is real and persistent

**What this means for the Seal Beach goal:**
At $5K/month ($60K/year), a Seal Beach purchase is 8-10 years out minimum — unless GG scales significantly or a different financing path is found (partnership, business income, equity-based deal).

---

## The Realistic Path

**Phase 1 (Current):** Stabilize income at $5K+/month, rebuild credit to 700+, eliminate debt.

**Phase 2 (1-2 years):** Move in with Brielle. Two incomes change the math substantially. Combined $10K+/month opens different options.

**Phase 3 (3-5 years):** Down payment accumulation. FHA loan minimum (3.5% down) on $850K = ~$30K. Conventional (10%) = $85K. Still large but achievable with a 3-5 year runway and growing GG income.

**Phase 4:** Seal Beach or equivalent coastal area. The dream is intact — the timeline is honest.

## Real Estate Market Dynamics

**Factors that could change the math:**
- Interest rate drops (significant impact on monthly payment)
- GG revenue growth (most controllable variable)
- Inheritance or windfall (unpredictable, don't plan around it)
- Market correction (possible but not reliable enough to plan for)

**Factors that won't change:**
- SoCal land scarcity — prices correct but rarely collapse
- Coastal premium — Seal Beach will always cost more than inland
- The credit score barrier — 700+ needed for best loan terms
