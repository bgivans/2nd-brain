# Design Inspiration

> A curated skill set built from studying 10 working designers. Use this before building client sites on Lovable, creating content for Givans Global, or directing any video/motion work. The goal is not to copy — it's to have a vocabulary.

---

## The Core Principle Before Anything Else

**Great design communicates before it impresses.**

Every designer below earns attention not by adding more, but by making intentional choices about what to remove, emphasize, or slow down. The skill to develop: **know what you're trying to make the viewer feel before you touch a tool.**

## Web & UI Design

### Nelson Noa
**Aesthetic:** Interactive, micro-animation-heavy, precision-coded UI
**Specialty:** Interfaces that respond to the user — hover states, transitions, scroll behavior as a design element
**What he does differently:** Every interaction is designed, not just every screen. The movement is the message.

**Apply to your work:**
- On Lovable builds: even simple hover color shifts on CTAs add perceived quality
- For Ellie's landing page: a subtle fade-in on the service section builds trust before the user reads a word
- Principle: **motion signals care** — it tells the visitor someone thought about them

---

### Tom Johnson
**Aesthetic:** Brutalist minimalism — raw grid, heavy type, negative space treated as a design element
**Specialty:** Typography-first layouts where the font IS the visual
**What he does differently:** Resists decoration. The restraint is the design.

**Apply to your work:**
- When in doubt on a Lovable build: less. More white space. Bigger type.
- For GG landing page: a single, heavy statement at the top of the fold ("We build the systems operators can't afford to skip") needs no image behind it
- Principle: **confidence communicates** — busy design signals insecurity

---

### Inga Hampton
**Aesthetic:** Editorial, magazine-quality layouts — image + type as co-equals
**Specialty:** Grid breaking done intentionally. Overlapping elements that feel editorial, not messy.
**What she does differently:** Borrows from print design. The eye has a path.

**Apply to your work:**
- Client content systems (Ellie, Javier, Byron): even social posts benefit from the editorial principle — one dominant image, one dominant line of text, nothing competing
- Principle: **create a reading order** — the viewer's eye should have exactly one place to start

---

### Gal Shir
**Aesthetic:** Product design, systematic color, subtle texture, purposeful illustration
**Specialty:** Building design systems — not just pages, but the rules that make pages consistent
**What he does differently:** Color is never random. Each palette has a hierarchy (dominant / secondary / accent in specific ratios)

**Apply to your work:**
- Before any client site: define 3 colors maximum — dominant, secondary, accent. Write it down before touching Lovable.
- For GG brand: currently under-systematized. Pick the palette once and commit.
- Principle: **system thinking applies to design** — the same instinct that builds CRM pipelines builds visual consistency

---

### Tatiana Tsiguleva
**Aesthetic:** Illustration-integrated UI — hand-drawn or semi-organic elements living inside digital interfaces
**Specialty:** Warmth in digital spaces. Personality that doesn't feel like a template.
**What she does differently:** Makes you feel like a real person made this, not a SaaS theme

**Apply to your work:**
- For clients in relationship-heavy niches (Ellie, Byron, Javier): a custom illustrated element — even a simple icon set — signals care that stock imagery can't
- Principle: **one unexpected human element breaks the template feel**

---

### Daniella Marynova
**Aesthetic:** Luxury, editorial, restrained elegance — fashion-world sensibility
**Specialty:** High-end positioning through design choices (spacing, type pairing, image treatment)
**What she does differently:** Makes premium feel effortless, not expensive

**Apply to your work:**
- When Givans Global is ready to move upmarket: the design signals should match the pricing. Right now GG reads mid-range. Luxury design is mostly about more space and fewer words.
- Principle: **price is communicated before the pricing page** — the design either validates or undercuts the number

## App & Motion Design

### Lee Black
**Aesthetic:** Cinematic dark UI — deep blacks, single accent color, editorial photography, film-grade color grading
**Specialty:** Dark mode done with intent. Atmosphere over brightness.
**What he does differently:** Treats UI like a film frame — every element has a reason to be in the shot

**Apply to your work:**
- Zooted Records aesthetic lives here: dark backgrounds, strong accent color (could be neon green, orange, or red), minimal text that punches
- Video content for any music client: study Lee Black's color grading approach — it's the difference between "shot on iPhone" and "shot with intent"
- Principle: **dark doesn't mean cheap** — it means every element has to justify its presence

---

### T. Costa (Thiago Costa)
**Aesthetic:** Motion and 3D integrated into flat design — depth and dimensionality on 2D surfaces
**Specialty:** Transitions that feel like physics — things move like they have weight
**What he does differently:** The motion tells the story. You understand the hierarchy of information because of how things move, not just where they sit

**Apply to your work:**
- Any video content: shots should feel like they have weight. Fast cuts are amateur. Intentional pacing with movement is professional.
- Zooted video content direction: think about how each element enters the frame — does it earn its appearance?
- Principle: **movement hierarchy** — what moves first is what matters most

---

### Gunnar Gray
**Aesthetic:** Bold typographic video — text as a primary visual element, not a caption
**Specialty:** Type in motion. Making words feel like events.
**What he does differently:** Words land like punches. Typography IS the cinematography.

**Apply to your work:**
- Givans Global content: most operators undersell with text. Big type on video makes the statement feel real.
- Social content for any client: one line at a time, with motion. More legible, more shareable, more memorable.
- Principle: **if the words are strong, let them be the hero** — don't compete with them

---

### Gabriel Foulon
**Aesthetic:** Surreal 3D — impossible objects, dream logic, product photography that bends reality
**Specialty:** Using 3D rendering to create images that couldn't exist in the physical world, but feel like they should
**What he does differently:** Earns attention by being genuinely strange, not just polished

**Apply to your work:**
- Byron / Furry Furrows: the 3D print crossover is an obvious application — product renders that show something impossible (a treat shaped like a parent's face, the dog inside a snow globe)
- Zooted Clothing: product mockups that feel more like art than merch photography
- Principle: **if your category is boring, be surreal** — strange is more shareable than polished

## Before Your Next Build — 3 Questions

1. **What is the one feeling I want the visitor to leave with?** (Not features. Feeling.)
2. **What am I removing?** Every design decision should cost you something — if you added it for free, cut it.
3. **Where does the eye go first?** If you can't answer this in 2 seconds, the layout isn't done yet.
