# Cover Letter — Texas 811 Operations Role

> Sent to John (hiring manager). See [[Projects/811 Texas - Prado]] for full context.

---

Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for an operations-focused role with Texas 811 because I specialize in building clear, reliable systems that help teams execute consistently and communicate expectations without friction. My background is in designing practical workflows that improve how information is captured, routed, and acted on, then making those workflows stick through documentation, training, and simple reporting.

In my work across real estate and legal operations, I have built CRM-based intake and routing processes, created SOPs that standardize handoffs, and implemented lightweight reporting that makes ownership and next actions obvious. The outcome is less confusion, fewer dropped follow-ups, and cleaner data that leaders can actually rely on. I am especially strong at taking ambiguous, fast-moving requirements and translating them into structured processes that teams can adopt without slowing down.

I am also AI-fluent and continuously up to date on applied AI for operational scale. I use modern tooling to reduce admin load, improve consistency in communication, and speed up documentation and knowledge sharing. I focus on practical, governed use cases that improve quality and throughput, not hype or experimentation for its own sake.

I want to be direct: I am new to the 811 and damage prevention domain. I am not applying because I already know everything about the ecosystem. I am applying because the core operational challenges are familiar: high volume, high consequence, time-sensitive workflows, multiple stakeholder types, and a need for clarity that prevents rework and frustration. I ramp quickly by interviewing stakeholders, mapping real workflows, documenting "what happens next," and iterating in small improvements that are easy for teams to adopt.

If hired, I would bring a disciplined approach to strengthening operational clarity:

- Identify where information quality breaks down at intake and handoffs, and tighten standards that reduce back-and-forth
- Improve routing and ownership visibility so the right team receives the right context fast
- Create SOPs and internal reference materials that reduce reliance on tribal knowledge
- Build simple reporting around throughput, resolution time, and the most common friction points so improvements can be measured

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss where Texas 811 is seeing the most friction today, and how I can contribute quickly while building domain expertise. Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
