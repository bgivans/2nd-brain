## Proposal Structure (from March 2026 — 27-message session)

The proposal is part of the sales process, not just a document. Key frameworks surfaced from a deep proposal strategy session:

**The three-part proposal structure that works:**
1. **The Mirror:** Show you understand their problem better than they articulated it. Repeat it back in sharper language. This creates instant trust.
2. **The Map:** Show specifically what you're going to build and in what order. Not a list of deliverables — a *sequence* with logic behind it.
3. **The Math:** Tie the investment to an outcome, not a price point. *"This landing page replaces a $1,500/month ad spend on organic traffic — it pays for itself in one month."*

**What kills proposals:**
- Leading with price before establishing value
- Vague scope ("I'll help with your marketing") — every word of scope must be specific
- No next step — the proposal should end with a clear decision point, not a "let me know what you think"
- Too many options — two choices max (do this or do that; more creates analysis paralysis)

**Campaign critique takeaways (from the April 2025 session):**
- When a client pushes back on price, they're usually saying "I don't see the value yet" — not "I can't afford it"
- The fix is specificity: exactly what deliverable, exactly what outcome, exactly what timeline
- A proposal that can't be answered yes or no in 60 seconds is too long
