## Body Composition

**Goal:** Cut body fat while maintaining / building muscle. Physique-focused, not just health-focused.

- Body fat estimation sessions in March and January 2026 tracked current baseline
- Target: visible definition — not obsessive, but intentional
- **Workout system:** → [[Systems/Training Split]] (Pull Day, Shoulder Day structure built out)
- Key metric: body weight is a lagging indicator — use mirror + performance as primary signals

### Post-Workout Nutrition

From January 2026 session:
- Protein within 30-60 min post-workout
- Target: **0.8–1g protein per lb of bodyweight daily**
- Carbs post-workout to replenish glycogen
- Caloric deficit for cut: ~300-500 below maintenance (not aggressive)
- Chicken, rice, eggs are the staples — no need to overcomplicate

## Morning Routine

From a December 2025 / January 2026 27-message session building out an actual routine:

**Target structure:**
1. Wake consistent time (goal: before 10am, even on late nights)
2. No phone for first 20-30 minutes — let the brain boot naturally
3. Hydration + light (sunlight or bright light) before caffeine
4. Neuro Gum or coffee after the first activity (not first thing)
5. Move before sitting — walk, stretch, or short workout before settling into work

**The challenge:** The 2-3pm natural activation makes early mornings feel unproductive. The morning routine isn't for peak output — it's for metabolic health and circadian rhythm anchoring.

## Sleep Strategy

- **Current:** Inconsistent, often late nights (1-3am, sometimes 5am)
- **Goal:** Earlier, more consistent sleep schedule — 11pm–7am target
- **Sleep improvement session (Feb 2026):** identified key levers:
  - Cut screens 30-60 min before sleep (hard with momentum-driven work)
  - Lower room temperature
  - Consistent wake time matters more than consistent bedtime
  - Magnesium glycinate before bed can help quality
- **Challenge:** Natural late activation + momentum-driven work sessions + ADHD medication on wrong schedule

**The medication timing issue:** Using ADHD medication late at night extends the work session but destroys sleep. The fix is to use it in the morning or afternoon — which requires shifting the work schedule. This is a behavioral system problem, not just a sleep hygiene problem.
