# Lead Generation Methods

> Synthesized from a January 10, 2025 session (13 messages) analyzing lead generation approaches — specifically for real estate-adjacent businesses but applicable across GG client types. Built to inform which methods are worth installing vs. which are noise.

---

## The Framework: Method × Intent × Effort

Not all lead gen methods are equal. The right question for each method is:
1. **Who is this targeting?** (intent quality)
2. **What does it cost to run?** (time + money)
3. **What volume can it realistically produce?** (scale)
4. **Can it be systematized?** (repeatability)

Methods that score high on intent quality and systematizability are worth building. Methods that require constant manual effort for uncertain output are traps.

---

## High-Intent Methods (Worth Systematizing)

**Cold Calling with Filtered Lists**
- Target: distressed sellers, expired listings, FSBO, probate leads
- Filtration is everything — raw calling lists are a waste of time
- Connected to: [[Systems/Urban Management - Calling Scripts]]
- Follow-up question from the session: *"With calling I will need methods of who can be targeted and what filtration methods are there"*

**Probate / Court Record Mining**
- High-intent by definition — someone has to handle the estate
- Source: county courthouse records, online probate databases
- Connected to: [[Projects/SD Probate Case Extraction]]

**Google Ads (Hormozi Landing Page)**
- Bottom-of-funnel, high commercial intent
- Works best when paired with a specific offer and a dedicated LP (not the main website)
- Connected to: [[GG_Hormozi_LP.md.md]]

**Referral Systems**
- Highest conversion rate, lowest cost per lead
- Requires existing client relationships to activate
- Gap: GG does not yet have a formalized referral system

---

## Medium-Intent Methods (Worth Testing)

**Content + SEO (Long-term)**
- Organic search captures people actively researching solutions
- Javier's ADU content is an example in progress
- Takes 3-6 months to produce meaningful results
- Connected to: [[Projects/Javier - Unlock Your Property]]

**Instagram / Social Outreach**
- Works for personal brand visibility, not direct lead gen at small volumes
- Best use: credibility building that makes cold outreach easier
- Connected to: [[Systems/Personal Brand Strategy]]

**GHL Automation + Drip**
- For nurturing leads that are not yet ready to buy
- Requires an existing list to work — not a lead gen method on its own
- Connected to: [[GG_Build_System]]

---

## Low-Intent Methods (Use Sparingly)

**Cold Email at Scale**
- Works but requires significant volume and a strong offer
- Spam risk, deliverability issues, high false positive rate
- Only worth it with proper list hygiene and a specific niche

**Door Knocking**
- High-effort, low volume, but very high intent if done well
- Connected to the door knocking sales pitch session (supplemented in Urban Management calling scripts)
- Best for hyper-local geographic campaigns

**Facebook Marketplace / Groups**
- Works for specific niches (real estate, home services)
- Not scalable, hard to systematize

## What the January 2025 Session Was Solving

The question at the time was: *"Where should I be targeting my lead gen efforts?"* — during the period when Urban Management was the primary client context and the real estate vertical was the core focus.

The answer then was: cold calling with filtered lists + probate mining + organic content. That's still the right answer for Urban Management-type clients.

---

## For GG as an Agency

GG's own lead gen problem is different:
- **Who is the ideal GG client?** Small businesses growing faster than their infrastructure — real estate, legal, trades, creative
- **Best method:** Warm referrals from existing clients + inbound from the GG website (when the Operator-in-a-Box page goes live)
- **Current gap:** No formalized referral mechanism, no inbound system running
