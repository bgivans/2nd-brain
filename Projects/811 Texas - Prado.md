## Interview Prep

### Vendor vs. Team Member Question
> "Are you looking for me as a **vendor delivering outcomes** or as a **team member embedded in operations**? I can do either. If the goal is full ownership and accountability, a short paid pilot is usually the cleanest way to prove it fast."

- If W2: "What's the expected weekly hours, and is there a clear path to full-time within 60–90 days?"
- If 1099: "Do you prefer a monthly retainer with defined outcomes, or a fixed-price sprint per project?"

### On Their Existing Website (Divi)
> "I can improve what's already there fast. Divi is fine when it's managed properly. I'll clean the content quality issues, tighten contractor pathways, implement tracking, and add targeted custom modules or lightweight tooling where it improves conversion. You get better performance without a full rebuild."

**Custom tooling examples that land with them:**
- Contractor Quick Start stepper (what to do, what happens next, escalation)
- Sticky click-to-call on mobile + state routing logic
- Structured FAQ blocks with schema markup
- "I'm a Contractor / Homeowner / Utility" router — gets people to the right flow fast

### Tools to Ask About
They likely use: Power BI, Excel, SQL Server, Azure, Salesforce/CRM, ticketing systems

## AI Angle for 811

Key framing if AI comes up in conversation:

**On long-horizon tasks:**
> "The 2026 shift is long-horizon tasks — AI is now beating human professionals 70.9% of the time on these. The question isn't if AI applies, it's what it handles and where human judgment stays in the loop."

**On damage prevention AI (directly relevant to their business):**
> "50% of all damages come from the top 8% of 'risky tickets.' AI's best current use isn't replacing judgment — it's optimizing when and where human expertise gets applied. Risk-based resource allocation."

**On MCP / tool integration:**
> "MCP (Model Context Protocol) is the new standard for connecting AI to any tool — like USB for AI integrations. If 811 isn't working with this yet, it's worth understanding — it's becoming the baseline expectation."

**Watch out:** Models can learn bad behaviors from humans. Training data quality matters — mimics the negligence it's trained on.
