# Thomas — Sauna Guy (Lost Lead)

**Role:** Prospect — did not convert
**Business:** Pressure washing (early stage, commercial acquisition focus)
**Met:** In the sauna, ~1 week ago
**Status:** Not ready — don't chase

---

## What Happened

Met Thomas in the sauna and offered my services. He pushed back via email.

### His Objections
- Business just starting out
- Price point too high
- Can handle website himself using AI tools
- Marketing scope limiting for the cost
- Wanted to see examples of past clients in commercial acquisition specifically

### My Response
- Acknowledged his concerns
- Offered to let him build front-end pieces himself
- Shared two case studies: C2C Document Preparation (CRM for 300+ clients), Unlock Your Property (lead research and organization)
- Linked to AI Automation page (Demo #2)
- Explained value framing (system, not just assets)
- Offered payment plan: 3x $1,000 instead of $3,000 upfront

No response after that. Not moving forward.

---

## Analysis

He's not wrong:
- Early-stage businesses often can't afford or justify $3K
- My examples weren't in his industry — he specifically asked for pressure washing / commercial acquisition
- AI tools make basic websites accessible, so my value needs to live in the system layer, not the asset layer

The pitch was solid but didn't address his core objection — no industry-specific proof.

---

## Lessons

1. **Don't chase** — he's not ready. If he comes back, engage. If not, move on.
2. **Need industry-specific case studies** or clearer positioning that the system-building skill transfers regardless of industry.
3. **The system layer is the moat** — if someone can DIY the website, the value pitch has to be what they can't DIY.
