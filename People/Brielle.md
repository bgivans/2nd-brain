## Long Distance Notes

From a March 2026 session on long-distance virtual date planning — applicable whenever the relationship has geographic distance:

**Virtual date ideas that actually work:**
- Simultaneous meal at the same time in different cities — coordinate delivery or cook the same recipe
- Watch a movie or show together (Teleparty / synced streaming)
- Online game together — even a simple game creates shared focus
- Virtual museum or gallery walk (many major museums have online collections)
- Cook together over FaceTime — same recipe, own kitchens, compete or collaborate
- Share a playlist and listen together while talking
- Send a care package timed to arrive for a planned "unboxing" call

**What makes virtual dates feel real:** shared activity > passive talking. Having something to *do* together creates the natural conversation that texting and video calls often miss.

## Pre-Marriage Prep

From an April 2025 session searching for pre-marriage seminars:

Pre-marriage seminars were being actively researched — not as a "have to" but as a deliberate investment in the relationship. The specific thing being looked for: structured conversations about life goals, communication, finances, and faith — not just relationship chemistry (already established).

Good pre-marriage programs cover:
- Financial expectations and money management as a couple
- Communication under stress
- Faith alignment and spiritual leadership
- Conflict resolution (beyond communication style)
- Family systems (what each person is bringing from their family of origin)

The Brielle faith dynamic (she wants God first — above her) makes a faith-integrated program the right fit, not a secular marriage course.
