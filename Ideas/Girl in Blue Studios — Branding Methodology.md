## My Take
Found someone doing branding the way it should be done — not just making things pretty, but building from the emotional brief outward. The pun naming thing is what caught my eye first, but the real insight is that the name, the product utility, and the marketing all point at the same thing. That's the part I want to install into how I think about my own projects. Not there yet on exactly how, but the three-step audit is a solid starting point.

## Ideas Explored
- Dissecting Anastasia / Girl in Blue Studios as a branding and marketing reference
- How she generates clever, punny business names that also communicate utility
- Why her campaigns feel cohesive — name, product, and marketing all aligned
- Emotional design as a strategic foundation (not just an aesthetic choice)
- Psychology-backed identity building — visual and verbal together
- Extracting her methodology into a reusable framework for my own startups

## What Has Legs
- The **emotional audit** as a starting point before naming or visuals: what does the customer feel before finding you, and what should they feel after?
- The **utility-first naming principle**: the pun works because it's accurate — it earns the cleverness by also being descriptive
- The **cohesion check**: does the name, visual, and marketing message all point to the same emotional promise?
- Her public content format ("If I were to create a [X] brand") as a way to demonstrate methodology without giving away client work — worth thinking about for my own content

## What to Kill or Park
- Don't get distracted by the aesthetic surface of her work — the real thing to steal is the sequencing (strategy first, visuals second)
- Park the "what if I hired her" line of thinking — this session is about extracting the method, not outsourcing it
