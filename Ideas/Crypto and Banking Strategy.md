# Crypto and Banking Strategy

> January 27, 2025. A 6-message session on moving money to stablecoins as an alternative to traditional banking. Short but worth preserving as a documented financial mindset moment.

---

## The Question Being Asked

*Should some of the cash float be held in stablecoins (USDC, USDT) rather than a traditional bank account?*

The context: January 2025, post-FTX collapse era, bank failures were still in recent memory (Silicon Valley Bank, March 2023). The concern wasn't speculation — it was **banking risk**. Stablecoins as a hedge against bank exposure, not as an investment.

---

## The Logic

**In favor of stablecoins for float:**
- USDC and USDT are pegged 1:1 to the dollar — no price risk on the principal
- Can earn 4-5% yield on stablecoin deposits (DeFi or centralized platforms like Coinbase)
- Removes exposure to bank counterparty risk
- Accessible 24/7, fast transfers, no wire fee delays

**Against (at the time):**
- Not FDIC insured
- Depeg risk (USDT depegged briefly in 2022)
- Tax implications on yield
- Operational complexity for a solo operator — more accounts to manage

---

## What This Reveals About the Financial Thinking

This session happened during a period when cash flow was tight and the security income was not yet locked in. The stablecoin question was not theoretical — it was someone trying to make $800 in a bank account work harder.

The instinct was right directionally: don't let cash sit idle, look for yield, understand the risk profile. The execution wasn't there yet.

---

## Current Relevance (March 2026)

As of March 2026, income is $2,018/month and bank balance is ~$800. The stablecoin strategy is not the priority right now — getting to $5K/month consistent is. But as income stabilizes, understanding where to hold cash, how to earn yield on a small float, and how to diversify banking exposure becomes more relevant.

File this as a *future financial infrastructure* note — not urgent, but worth returning to when the income gap is closed.
