# Custom GPT Concepts — Early 2025

> Two sessions in January 2025 that planted the seeds for the current custom agent work — specifically Javier's bot, Ellie's bot, and the Objection Crusher GPT. Historical capture of early ideation.

---

## Session 1 — Jan 8: Custom GPTs for Growth

A session exploring how custom GPTs could accelerate client business growth. The focus at this point was still conceptual — what could a custom GPT do for a small business?

The framework being developed:
- A GPT that knows the client's products, pricing, and FAQs can handle initial inquiries
- A GPT trained on objection-handling can coach sales reps mid-call
- A GPT with scheduling and follow-up logic can reduce admin drag

These were theoretical. The clients weren't yet defined at this level of granularity.

---

## Session 2 — Jan 27: Custom Bot Ideas (for Javier's Businesses)

This session got specific:

> *"Come up with custom bot ideas for Javier and his businesses."*

Three follow-up bots were considered for the Javier / Unlock Your Property context. The follow-up question — *"How much time and money would each save me?"* — shows the ROI framing was already in place.

This is the origin conversation for:
- The Javier client bot (ADU-focused, real estate inquiry handling)
- The concept that became the Objection Crusher GPT (→ [[Systems/Objection Crusher GPT]])

---

## The Pattern

Both sessions share the same structure: identify a problem → imagine a GPT that solves it → ask what it's worth. That's the right order. The mistake most people make is building the GPT before asking what it's worth.

By March 2025, the Objection Crusher GPT design session (→ [[Systems/Objection Crusher GPT]]) was more specific and implemented. These January sessions are the ideation layer that made that possible.
