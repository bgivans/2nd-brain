# AI Content Creation SaaS

> February 12, 2025. A session specifically on building a SaaS product around AI content creation — distinct from Automated Content System (the internal GG tool) and more focused on a sellable product.

---

## The Concept

A SaaS platform where businesses or creators input their brand voice, niche, and goals — and the platform generates a library of content ready to schedule and post. Not a one-off generator. A persistent system that learns the brand over time.

The differentiation from generic AI writing tools: **context persistence**. Most AI content tools treat every generation as a blank slate. This would maintain brand memory — your tone, your offers, your audience, your past posts — and improve over time.

---

## Why It Was Interesting in February 2025

February 2025 was peak "AI content" hype. Every agency was promising AI-generated content. Most were wrapping ChatGPT with thin UX and calling it a product.

The bet here was: the real product isn't the generation — it's the **context layer** that makes the generation feel like it knows the brand. That's a harder technical problem and a harder UX problem. It's also a moat.

---

## The Business Model

- **Starter:** $49/month — generate X posts/month, basic brand inputs
- **Growth:** $149/month — full content calendar, scheduling, multiple platforms
- **Agency:** $499/month — multiple brands, white-label output, client-facing dashboard

The agency tier is where GG would be the buyer — a tool to serve clients more efficiently, not a personal content tool.

---

## The Reality Check

By mid-2025, the AI content tool market was commoditizing fast. Jasper, Copy.ai, Lately, and a dozen others were competing on price. The context-persistence angle is real, but building it requires substantial ML infrastructure that's out of scope for a solo operator.

The more viable path: don't build the SaaS, but build the **workflow** — a proprietary process using existing AI tools (Claude, Perplexity) that produces context-aware content for GG clients. That's already partially happening.
