# Log — December 2025 — Career Research (Data Scientist Roles)

> **Archive log.** Situational — documents a moment of considering a career pivot toward data science roles. Late 2025. Preserved as context for the mindset during a period of GG uncertainty.

---

## What Was Being Researched

December 1, 2025. A session on data scientist roles and salary ranges.

This was not abstract curiosity. December 2025 was likely a period of GG income uncertainty — researching what a W2 alternative might look like if the agency path stalled. Data science sits at the intersection of Bryce's actual skill set:
- AI fluency
- Systems thinking
- Pattern recognition across datasets
- Tool-building instinct

---

## What the Research Revealed

**Data scientist roles (late 2025):**
- Entry-level: $85,000–$110,000/year (typically requires ML experience + formal education or portfolio)
- Mid-level: $120,000–$160,000/year
- Senior: $160,000–$220,000+

**The gap:** Data scientist roles typically require:
- Python proficiency (statistical modeling, pandas, scikit-learn)
- SQL (strong — this aligns with current skill set)
- Statistics background (gap — not formal education)
- Portfolio of ML projects (gap — no public data science work)

**The aligned path:** AI systems integration or AI product management would be more immediately accessible than pure data science — the systems and business instincts are there, the data science technical depth isn't without investment.

---

## What This Moment Reveals

December 2025 career research alongside active GG work is a signal: the agency was not yet at a stability point where the W2 question was fully off the table. The income ceiling anxiety that drove this research is the same thing that drove the 811 Texas research in January–February 2026.

The underlying pattern: when GG income is uncertain, W2 research spikes. When GG income is stable, the research stops. The research itself is a diagnostic signal about GG's financial state.

## Current Read (March 2026)

The data science pivot was not pursued. The operator path continued. The AI skills being developed through GG work are more valuable in context than they would be as a data scientist credential.
