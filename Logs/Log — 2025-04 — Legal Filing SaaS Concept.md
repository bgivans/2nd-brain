# Log — April 2025 — Legal Filing SaaS Concept

> April 5, 2025. A single-message session outlining a business plan for a legal filing SaaS. Connected to the C2C Legal / Ellie relationship that was active at the time. Historical — label as shelved or evolved into current C2C work.

---

## The Concept

A SaaS platform that manages the legal filing workflow for small legal service businesses — specifically the kind of paralegal-adjacent work that C2C Legal (Ellie's operation) handles:

- Document intake and organization
- Vendor coordination (the session referenced: *"You also need to manage all of our different vendors and so that we just have all the contacts lined up and squared away and organized"*)
- Filing deadline tracking
- Client communication

The operational problem: legal service businesses often have multiple vendors (process servers, court clerks, filing services, attorneys), and the coordination between them is manual, error-prone, and doesn't scale.

---

## Why This Was Being Explored

April 2025 — deep in the C2C Legal relationship with Ellie. The Ellie project involved building systems for her legal compliance business. At some point the question became: is the system we're building for Ellie a custom installation, or is it a product that other legal service businesses would pay for?

The SaaS version of that answer would be: package the workflow as a product, not a service.

---

## Why It's Shelved

1. **Building software for legal businesses requires legal domain expertise** — Ellie is the domain expert, but she's a client, not a co-founder
2. **The compliance and liability risk is high** — a missed filing deadline in a legal context has consequences that a general business tool doesn't
3. **The market is niche and already served** — Clio, MyCase, Practice Panther, and others compete in legal practice management
4. **GG's current model (service delivery, not SaaS) was the right call** — deliver the system install for Ellie, not build software competing against funded legal tech companies
