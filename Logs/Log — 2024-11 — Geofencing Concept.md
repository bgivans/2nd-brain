# Log — November 2024 — AI Geofencing for Motivated Sellers

> **Pre-GG era.** This is the origin of the real estate AI thinking — before the agency model, before the current client list, before the stack was clear. Historical reference only.

---

## The Concept

**AI geofencing for motivated sellers** — using location-based targeting to identify and reach homeowners in financial distress or in high-probability-of-sale zones.

The idea: cross-reference geofencing data (mobile location signals near certain addresses) with property data (distress indicators, tax liens, probate filings, foreclosure status) to build hyper-targeted outreach lists for real estate investors and agents.

This is November 2024 — before the SD Probate Case Extraction project (→ [[Projects/SD Probate Case Extraction]]) was formalized, before Urban Management was a named client. The geofencing concept predates both.

---

## Why It's Worth Preserving

This conversation documents the **moment the real estate + AI thesis started forming**. The thinking at the time:
- Real estate has abundant public data but terrible data activation
- AI can close the gap between "data exists" and "someone acts on it"
- The buyer for this is not a homeowner — it's the investor or agent who wants motivated sellers delivered to them

That thesis is still alive in the current work:
- SD Probate Case Extraction (→ [[Projects/SD Probate Case Extraction]])
- Urban Management lead systems
- Javier / Unlock Your Property ADU strategy

---

## What Didn't Happen

The geofencing concept as pitched in November 2024 was never built as a standalone product. The direction shifted toward building tools for specific clients rather than building a data product for the broader market.

Whether this concept ever becomes its own product depends on whether GG moves toward data services (possible, given the North Star consulting + venture direction).

---

## Era Note

November 2024 is before the studio exit, before the white label AI phase, before the restructure. This is early-thinking Bryce — not scattered, but not yet assembled. The ideas were forming. The business wasn't.
