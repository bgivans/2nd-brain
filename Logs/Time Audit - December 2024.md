# Time Audit — December 2024

> A snapshot of how Bryce was scoring and allocating time in December 2024. The actual scoring tables didn't survive the Milanote export, but the analysis is preserved here. Interesting as a historical baseline.

---

## The Framework (Worth Keeping)

Bryce was evaluating weekly activities across multiple dimensions — not just time spent, but:
- Financial benefit
- Health impact
- Personal growth
- Enjoyment
- Time cost

And critically: **tracking interdependencies between activities** — recognizing that the value of one block depended on another.

This is a more honest way to audit a calendar than simple time-blocking. Most people optimize for time. This was optimizing for *value per hour* with compounding effects accounted for.

## The Interdependency Insight

The analysis flagged that **SSD & FB Meetings** (Soulshakedown / Final Bounce era) had compound value that wasn't obvious on the surface:
- Podcasts depended on them → they inherited podcast value
- ~25% of freelance work sourced from those meetings → they had income value

The lesson: **some activities look low-value in isolation but are upstream of high-value outputs.** Cutting them without tracing the dependency first is a mistake.
